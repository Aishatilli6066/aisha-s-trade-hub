Cargo moves on paper as much as it moves on ships. Get the documents wrong and your container sits at port accruing demurrage at USD 150 per day.

## The core stack

- **Commercial Invoice** — buyer, seller, Incoterms, unit price, total value.
- **Packing List** — carton count, weights, dimensions, container number.
- **Bill of Lading (B/L)** — title document. Whoever holds the original B/L owns the cargo.
- **Certificate of Origin** — needed for preferential duty rates.
- **Phytosanitary / Health Certificate** — for agricultural and food cargo.
- **Insurance certificate** — under CIF/CIP terms.

## Incoterms matter

EXW, FOB, CIF, DDP — each shifts the cost and risk transfer point. Negotiate the Incoterm before you negotiate the price; the same USD 4.00 means very different things at FOB vs CIF.

## The clause that saves accounts

"Original B/L released only against full payment" — written into the contract and confirmed with your freight forwarder. Without it, an unscrupulous buyer can clear cargo before paying.
