The fastest way to lose money in international trade is to wire a 30% deposit to a supplier you have not verified. Here is the verification stack I use on every engagement.

## 1. Confirm the legal entity

Ask for the **Business License** (营业执照). Cross-check the registration number on the official National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System. Verify the registered scope actually includes manufacturing of your product category — many "factories" on B2B platforms are trading companies.

## 2. Independent factory audit

A live video walk-through is the minimum. For orders over USD 20,000, a third-party on-site audit (SGS, QIMA, or a vetted local inspector) is non-negotiable. Audit the production line, raw material storage, and QC bench — not just the showroom.

## 3. Sample protocol

Pay for samples. Free samples bias the seller toward their best output. Inspect the sample against your spec sheet, not against the seller's photos.

## 4. Payment terms that protect you

- 30% deposit, 70% against copy of Bill of Lading is the standard for trusted suppliers.
- For first orders, escrow via Alibaba Trade Assurance or an LC at sight is safer.
- Never wire 100% upfront. Ever.

## 5. Pre-shipment inspection

Book a PSI before the balance payment leaves your account. A USD 300 inspection has saved clients USD 40,000 orders more times than I can count.
