Nigeria is one of the world's largest sesame producers. The export opportunity is real — and the operational details are where most first-time exporters fail.

## Sourcing

Jigawa, Benue, Nasarawa, and parts of Kano produce export-grade sesame. Buy from licensed aggregators with cleaning capacity; field-purchased seed almost always fails moisture and FFA specs.

## Quality grades

International buyers typically request: **99/1/1 hulled** or **98/2/2 natural**, moisture ≤ 7%, FFA ≤ 2%. Get a SGS or Cotecna certificate of analysis before shipment.

## Documentation stack

- NEPC exporter registration
- NXP form (Nigeria Export Proceeds)
- Phytosanitary certificate (NAQS)
- Certificate of origin (NACCIMA / Chamber of Commerce)
- Commercial invoice & packing list
- Bill of Lading

## Payment terms

For first-time buyers, **LC at sight** confirmed by a tier-1 bank is the only safe route. Avoid "CAD" (cash against documents) until you've shipped to the same buyer at least twice cleanly.

## What kills deals

Moisture spikes during the rainy season. FFA creeping above 2% during slow shipment. Container fumigation skipped at port. Each of these is a 5–15% claim against your invoice.
