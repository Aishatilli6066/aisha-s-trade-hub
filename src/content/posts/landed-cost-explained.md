Landed cost is the all-in cost to deliver one unit to your warehouse, ready to sell. Get this wrong and you'll discount yourself out of business.

## The components

1. **FOB unit price** — what the supplier charges, loaded on the vessel.
2. **Ocean / air freight** — quote per container or per kg, varies wildly by lane and season.
3. **Marine insurance** — typically 0.3–0.5% of CIF value.
4. **Import duty** — country and HS-code specific.
5. **VAT / consumption tax** — applied on CIF + duty.
6. **Port charges & terminal handling** — fixed plus per-container.
7. **Customs clearance & agent fees**.
8. **Inland haulage** to your warehouse.
9. **Financing cost** — money tied up from deposit to sale.

## Worked example

A USD 4.00 FOB unit, 5,000 units in a 40HQ container to Lagos, can land at USD 5.40–6.20 depending on freight rates and clearance efficiency. That is a 35–55% uplift on FOB. Sellers who price off FOB always underprice.

## The discipline

Build a landed-cost model in a spreadsheet **before** you place the PO. Re-run it the day shipment lands. The variance is your real margin lesson.
