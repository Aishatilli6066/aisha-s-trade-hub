**OEM** (Original Equipment Manufacturer) means the factory builds to your design and spec. **ODM** (Original Design Manufacturer) means you brand the factory's existing design.

## Choose OEM when

- You own the design, IP, or a patented mechanism.
- You need a unique form factor competitors cannot copy off-the-shelf.
- Your brand sells on differentiation, not price.

## Choose ODM when

- You're testing a market and need a 90-day, low-MOQ launch.
- The category is commoditized (chargers, cables, basic kitchenware).
- Your edge is brand, packaging, and distribution — not the product itself.

## What to negotiate either way

- **Tooling ownership** (OEM): the mold belongs to you, in writing.
- **Exclusivity windows** (ODM): no competitor sells your exact SKU in your market for X months.
- **NNN agreement** before sharing any design files. NDAs are not enough for China.
